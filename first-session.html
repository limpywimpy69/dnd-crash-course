Module 6: Your First Session - What to Expect

You've learned the rules. You've created a character. You're almost ready! Here's what to expect when you actually sit down to play.

What is Session Zero?

Many groups start with a "Session Zero" before playing. This is where you:

  • Meet the other players
  • Discuss what kind of game everyone wants (serious? silly? combat-heavy? roleplay-heavy?)
  • Establish table rules and boundaries (topics to avoid, content ratings, etc.)
  • Create characters together (so you can build a cohesive party)
  • Learn about the setting and starting situation

Session Zero is optional, but highly recommended for new groups!

What to Bring

Required:

  • Your character sheet (printed or digital)
  • Dice (or a dice roller app)
  • Pencil and eraser
  • Paper for notes

Optional but helpful:

  • Snacks or drinks to share
  • Miniature or token to represent your character (if using a battle grid)
  • Spell cards (if you're a spellcaster)
  • A notebook for tracking party info and quest details

Most importantly: bring a positive attitude and willingness to collaborate!

Table Etiquette

DO:

  • Pay attention during others' turns
  • Share the spotlight—let everyone have their moments
  • Ask questions if you're confused
  • Say "yes" to your fellow players' ideas when possible
  • Take notes on important NPCs and plot points
  • Respect the DM's rulings (discuss rules disputes later, not mid-session)

DON'T:

  • Hog all the attention
  • Check your phone constantly
  • Argue over rules during the game
  • Derail the story for no reason
  • Be mean to other players (even in character)

Common Beginner Mistakes (And How to Avoid Them)

Mistake 1: Over-planning
Your elaborate 10-step plan will fall apart when the dice don't cooperate. Be flexible!

Mistake 2: Analysis Paralysis
Don't spend 10 minutes deciding what to do. When in doubt, just try something!

Mistake 3: Forgetting Modifiers
Always add your modifiers to rolls. Write them clearly on your character sheet!

Mistake 4: Not Using Class Features
Read your class abilities and actually USE them. Fighters get Second Wind, Rogues get Cunning Action—don't forget!

Mistake 5: Playing Against the Party
D&D is cooperative. "My character would steal from the party" is usually not fun for anyone.

Where to Find Games

In-Person:

  • Local game stores (many host D&D Adventurers League games)
  • University/college gaming clubs
  • Libraries and community centers
  • Ask friends if they're interested in starting a group!

Online:

  • r/lfg subreddit (Looking For Group)
  • Roll20, Foundry VTT, or Fantasy Grounds
  • D&D Beyond's Looking for Party forum
  • Discord servers dedicated to finding groups

Adventurers League is official D&D organized play—great for beginners because you can drop in/out of games.

What to Expect in Your First Session

Reality check:

  • You probably won't get through much story—that's okay!
  • You'll forget rules—everyone does, even veterans
  • Combat will take longer than you expect
  • You might feel awkward—that's normal and temporary
  • The DM might make mistakes too—be patient
  • Unexpected things WILL happen—embrace the chaos!

Focus on:

  • Having fun with friends
  • Learning as you go
  • Telling a story together
  • Not worrying about "playing perfectly"
