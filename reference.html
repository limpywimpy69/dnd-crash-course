📖 Glossary: Common D&D Terms

Ability Check: Rolling a d20 + ability modifier to see if you succeed at a task.
Ability Modifier: The bonus (or penalty) from an ability score, calculated as (Score - 10) ÷ 2.
Ability Score: Your character's core stats (STR, DEX, CON, INT, WIS, CHA), usually 3-20.
AC (Armor Class): How hard you are to hit. Higher is better.
Action: The main thing you do on your turn in combat (Attack, Cast a Spell, etc.).
Advantage: Roll 2d20 and use the higher result.
Bonus Action: A quick action some abilities let you take in addition to your main action.
Campaign: A series of connected D&D sessions telling one big story.
CHA (Charisma): Ability score representing force of personality and social influence.
CON (Constitution): Ability score representing health, stamina, and endurance.
Critical Hit: Rolling a natural 20 on an attack—hit automatically and roll damage dice twice!
Critical Fail: Rolling a natural 1 on an attack—automatic miss.
d20: A 20-sided die. The main die used in D&D.
DC (Difficulty Class): The target number you need to meet or beat on an ability check or save.
DEX (Dexterity): Ability score representing agility, reflexes, and coordination.
Disadvantage: Roll 2d20 and use the lower result.
DM (Dungeon Master): The person who runs the game, narrates the world, and plays NPCs.
HP (Hit Points): Your health. When you reach 0, you fall unconscious.
Initiative: d20 + DEX modifier, rolled at the start of combat to determine turn order.
INT (Intelligence): Ability score representing reasoning, memory, and logic.
Long Rest: 8 hours of rest that fully restores HP and abilities.
Natural 20/1: The number shown on the d20 itself (before adding modifiers).
NPC (Non-Player Character): Any character controlled by the DM (not a player).
PC (Player Character): A character controlled by a player.
Proficiency Bonus: A bonus (+2 at Level 1) added to rolls for things you're trained in.
Reaction: An instant response to a trigger, taken outside your turn (like opportunity attacks).
Round: In combat, one cycle through all combatants' turns (~6 seconds in game time).
Saving Throw: A d20 roll to resist or avoid an effect (spells, traps, poison, etc.).
Short Rest: About 1 hour of rest to catch your breath and recover some abilities.
Spell Slot: A resource spellcasters use to cast spells. Once used, must rest to recover.
STR (Strength): Ability score representing physical power and muscle.
WIS (Wisdom): Ability score representing awareness, intuition, and insight.

THE SIX ABILITIES

  • STR (Strength): Physical power
  • DEX (Dexterity): Agility and reflexes
  • CON (Constitution): Endurance and health
  • INT (Intelligence): Reasoning and memory
  • WIS (Wisdom): Awareness and insight
  • CHA (Charisma): Force of personality

WHAT YOU CAN DO ON YOUR TURN

  • Movement: Up to your speed (usually 30 ft)
  • Action: Attack, Cast Spell, Dash, Disengage, Dodge, Help, Hide, etc.
  • Bonus Action: Only if you have an ability that uses one
  • Free Interaction: Draw weapon, open door, shout, etc.
  • Reaction: (on someone else's turn) Opportunity attack, etc.

MAKING AN ATTACK

  1. Roll d20 + ability modifier + proficiency bonus
  2. Compare to target's AC
  3. If hit: Roll weapon damage + ability modifier
  4. Target subtracts damage from HP

ADVANTAGE & DISADVANTAGE

  • Advantage: Roll 2d20, use HIGHER
  • Disadvantage: Roll 2d20, use LOWER

COMMON DICE

  • d4: Pyramid (4 sides)
  • d6: Cube (6 sides)
  • d8: Octahedron (8 sides)
  • d10: Pentagonal trapezohedron (10 sides)
  • d12: Dodecahedron (12 sides)
  • d20: Icosahedron (20 sides)
  • d100 (percentile): Roll two d10s (one for tens, one for ones)

AT 0 HIT POINTS

  • Fall unconscious
  • Make death saving throws each turn:
    • 10+ = success
    • 9 or less = failure
    • 3 successes = stabilized
    • 3 failures = dead
  • Allies can heal you or stabilize you (Medicine check DC 10)

🔗 Resources for Further Learning

Official Rules & Tools

  • D&D Basic Rules (FREE PDF): https://www.dndbeyond.com/sources/basic-rules
  • D&D Beyond: Free character creation tool and digital rulebooks
  • Player's Handbook: The core rulebook ($29.99 on Amazon or local game store)

Finding Games

  • r/lfg (Reddit): Find groups looking for players
  • Roll20: Free virtual tabletop for online games
  • D&D Adventurers League: Official organized play (find a store near you)
  • Local Game Stores: Many host weekly D&D nights

Learning by Watching

  • Critical Role: Professional voice actors play D&D (popular but LONG episodes)
  • Dimension 20: Shorter, funnier actual-play series
  • Dungeons & Daddies: Comedy D&D podcast (not about actual dads)

Community & Help

  • r/DnD: General D&D subreddit
  • r/DMAcademy: Advice for Dungeon Masters
  • D&D Beyond Forums: Official community
