Module 1: What is D&D?

Dungeons & Dragons (D&D) is a tabletop roleplaying game where a group of players tell stories together—a mix of improvisational theatre, collaborative storytelling, and dice-rolling strategy. One player acts as the Dungeon Master (DM), while the others play as heroes in a fantasy world.

The Dungeon Master: The Narrator

The DM is like the author and referee of the game. They:

  • Describe the world, locations, and events the players encounter
  • Play all the other characters in the world (called NPCs—Non-Player Characters)
  • Create (or use pre-made) adventures for you to experience
  • Decide when you need to roll dice to see if you succeed at something
  • Make final rulings on rules questions

The DM isn't "against" the players—they're there to help everyone have fun and tell an exciting story together.

The Players: The Heroes of the Story

As a player, you create a character—maybe a brave knight, a sneaky rogue, or a powerful wizard. This character is who you'll "be" during the game. You'll:

  • Describe what your character does and says
  • Make decisions (Should we fight the dragon or try to sneak past?)
  • Roll dice when the outcome is uncertain
  • Work with other players to overcome challenges

What Makes D&D Different from Video Games?

In a video game, you can only do what the programmers planned for. In D&D, you can try ANYTHING. Want to convince the villain to join your side? Climb on the dragon's back? Open a bakery instead of going on adventures? You can try! The DM will help figure out if and how it works.

What Does a Session Look Like?

A typical D&D session lasts 3-4 hours (though some are shorter or longer). Here's roughly what happens:

  1. Recap: The DM reminds everyone what happened last time
  2. The Adventure: The DM describes situations, you make choices, roll dice for uncertain actions
  3. Combat (maybe): If you fight enemies, combat has structured turns
  4. Roleplay: Talk to NPCs, make plans, interact with the world
  5. Breaks: Snack breaks are important!

There's No "Winning"

D&D isn't about beating other players or reaching "the end." It's about the journey, the story, and the fun you have together. Some of the best D&D moments come from failure—like when your plan goes hilariously wrong or your character does something ridiculous.
